​ Solar system

The Solar System is made up of the Sun and all of the smaller objects that move around it. 
​Apart from the Sun, the largest members of the Solar System are the eight major planets.​

read more

Mercury

Mercury is the first planet from the Sun. The planet speeds around the Sun once every 88 days. However, it spins on its axis very slowly – once every 58.6 days. It has no atmosphere and no water. The noon temperature at the equator can soar to 450°C but the nights are extremely cold, below -180°C.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun, so it is always fairly close to the Sun in the sky. 
It is blanketed with a thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide.
The planet’s temperature soars to 465°C.

Earth

The Earth zooms around the Sun.
It takes 365 days (one year) to complete one orbit of the Sun.
The tilt of the axis that joins the north and south poles means that the Earth has seasons. 
When the north pole points towards the Sun, then it is summer in the northern countries, and vice versa.

Moon

The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite and the fifth largest moon in the solar system.
The Moon’s presence helps stabilize our planet’s wobble and moderate our climate.
The Moon’s distance from Earth is about 240,000 miles

Mars

Mars is often called the 'Red Planet' because it appears in the sky as an orange-red star. 
Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun. It orbits the Sun at an average distance of 228 million km.
The air is 100 times thinner than on Earth, and mostly made up of carbon dioxide.
Violent storms can even whip up clouds of dust!

Jupiter

Beyond the asteroid belt is Jupiter, the fifth planet from the Sun. Everything about Jupiter is large. It is so big that it could easily swallow all of the other planets.
Jupiter is five times as far from the Sun as the Earth, so its surface temperature is low, around –145°C.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun.
Saturn is made mainly of the light gases hydrogen and helium. 764 Earths would fit inside Saturn.
Saturn spins once in a little more than 10 hours.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun.
Uranus lies more than 2,800 million km from the Sun. At this distance, the temperature of its cloud tops is -214 degrees C. It moves quite slowly and has a long way to travel, so each orbit lasts 84 years.
64 Earths would fit inside it. Despite its size, it spins rapidly. A day on Uranus lasts only 17 hours 14 minutes.
